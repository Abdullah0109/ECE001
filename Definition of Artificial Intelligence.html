Throughout computer science, artificial intelligence, also called machine intelligence, is the intelligence displayed by computers, as opposed to the actual intelligence demonstrated by humans and animals. Artificial intelligence is described as the "smart agents" study: any system that knows its environment and takes steps that increase its chances of achieving its goals successfully. Colloquially, the term "artificial intelligence" is sometimes used to describe devices (or computers) that imitate the "cognitive" functions people associate with the human mind, such as "thinking" and "problem solving."
